## Question 1: Michelson Interferometer and Signal Modulation

An FTIR spectrometer is being used to analyze a polymer sample. The instrument uses a Michelson interferometer with a moving mirror that travels at a constant velocity of 0.25 cm/s.

**Given:**
- Mirror velocity: $v = 0.25$ cm/s
- The relationship between modulated frequency ($f_{\text{mod}}$), light frequency ($\nu$), and mirror velocity is: 
$$f_{\text{mod}} = \frac{2\nu v}{c}$$
- Speed of light: $c = 3.0 \times 10^{10}$ cm/s

**a)** A strong C=O stretching absorption occurs at 1720 cm⁻¹. Calculate the frequency (in Hz) of this infrared radiation.

**b)** For this same C=O absorption, what is the modulated frequency (in Hz) that will appear in the interferogram?

**c)** Explain in 2-3 sentences how the Michelson interferometer creates the interference pattern that is detected.

**d)** If the mirror velocity were doubled to 0.50 cm/s, what would happen to the modulated frequency for the 1720 cm⁻¹ absorption? (circle one)

- It would be halved
- It would remain the same  
- It would be doubled
- It would be quadrupled

**e)** An O-H stretching vibration appears at 3.0 μm. Express this same absorption in units of:
   - **i)** wavenumbers (cm⁻¹)
   - **ii)** frequency (Hz)

## Question 2: FT-IR Instrumentation and Performance

A research laboratory is deciding between purchasing a dispersive IR spectrometer or an FT-IR spectrometer for routine organic compound characterization and trace analysis.

**Given:**
- Dispersive instrument: scans from 4000–400 cm⁻¹ in 5 minutes, single scan
- FT-IR instrument: collects full spectrum (4000–400 cm⁻¹) in 1 second per scan
- Budget allows for either instrument
- Typical samples include: polymer additives at ppm levels, pharmaceutical compounds, environmental contaminants

**a)** List three distinct advantages of FT-IR spectrometry compared to dispersive IR instruments.

**b)** In the FT-IR instrument, a complete interferogram is collected in 1 second. The first scan yields a signal-to-noise ratio of 3:1 for a weak absorption band. If 16 scans are collected and averaged, what will be the approximate signal-to-noise ratio?

**c)** How much total time would be required to collect the 16 scans in part (b) using the FT-IR? How does this compare to a single scan using the dispersive instrument?

**d)** The Fellgett (multiplex) advantage is one of the key benefits of FT-IR. Which statement BEST describes this advantage? (circle one)

- All wavelengths are measured simultaneously, improving speed and signal-to-noise ratio
- The interferometer has no moving parts, making it more reliable
- The instrument can measure both near-IR and mid-IR simultaneously  
- The Fourier transform algorithm removes all noise from the spectrum

**e)** A colleague argues that for routine analysis of high-concentration samples where speed doesn't matter, a dispersive IR would be just as good as FT-IR. In one paragraph (3-5 sentences), explain why FT-IR would still likely be the better choice even in this scenario.
