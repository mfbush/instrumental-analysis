Question 1: Detector Response and Beer's Law Calculations

A portable photometer with a linear response to radiation is being used to determine the concentration of iron(III) in groundwater samples. The photometer measures current (in μA) that is directly proportional to the radiant power striking the detector.

Given:

  • The instrument was first zeroed with no light striking the detector
  • With pure water in the sample holder, the photometer registered 82.5 μA
  • A standard solution containing 5.00 ppm Fe³⁺ (complexed with thiocyanate to form the red Fe(SCN)₂⁺ complex) gave a reading of 23.8 μA
  • An unknown groundwater sample, after identical treatment with thiocyanate, gave a reading of 48.6 μA
  • Measurements were made at 480 nm
  • Path length = 1.00 cm

Calculate:

a) The percent transmittance of the standard solution

b) The absorbance of the standard solution

c) The concentration of Fe³⁺ in the unknown groundwater sample (in ppm)

d) What current reading would you expect for a sample containing 7.50 ppm Fe³⁺?

e) Based on the wavelength of maximum absorption (480 nm), what color would you expect to observe for the Fe(SCN)₂⁺ complex? Briefly explain your reasoning.
